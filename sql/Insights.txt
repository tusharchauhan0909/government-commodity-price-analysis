# Key Insights – Government Commodity Price Analysis

## Insight 1: High-Value Commodities
Spices and premium commodities such as cumin seed have significantly higher average modal
prices compared to vegetables, indicating higher market value and stronger demand.

---

## Insight 2: Regional Price Differences
Average commodity prices vary considerably across states, suggesting regional
supply-demand imbalances, transportation costs, and differences in market access.

---

## Insight 3: Price Volatility
Certain commodities show high price volatility, as reflected by large differences between
minimum and maximum prices. These commodities carry higher income risk for farmers
and higher trading risk for businesses.

---

## Insight 4: Seasonal Trends
Monthly trend analysis reveals noticeable price fluctuations over time, indicating
seasonality in agricultural commodity prices influenced by harvest cycles and demand patterns.

---

## Insight 5: Market-Level Pricing
Some mandis consistently report higher average prices, likely due to stronger buyer presence,
better infrastructure, or proximity to urban centers. These markets can offer better returns
to farmers.

---

## Insight 6: Price Stability vs Risk
Staple commodities tend to have more stable prices, making them lower-risk options,
while premium and specialty commodities exhibit higher volatility, offering higher
returns but increased risk.

---

## Overall Conclusion
The analysis demonstrates that agricultural commodity prices are influenced by commodity type,
region, seasonality, and market characteristics. These insights can support better decision-making
for farmers, traders, and policymakers by balancing profitability, risk, and market access.
