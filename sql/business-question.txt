# Business Questions – Government Commodity Price Analysis

## Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze government mandi (market) price data to
understand commodity pricing trends, regional differences, and price volatility.
The analysis aims to derive insights that can support farmers, traders, and policymakers
in making data-driven decisions.

---

## Business Questions

### Q1. Which commodities have the highest average modal price?
- Identify high-value commodities across markets.
- Helps understand which commodities generate higher revenue potential.

---

### Q2. How do average commodity prices vary across states?
- Analyze regional price differences.
- Helps identify states with higher or lower average pricing, indicating supply-demand imbalance.

---

### Q3. Which commodities show the highest price volatility?
- Measure risk by analyzing the difference between minimum and maximum prices.
- Helps identify commodities with unstable pricing.

---

### Q4. What is the monthly price trend of commodities over time?
- Analyze how prices change month by month.
- Helps identify seasonality and time-based patterns in commodity prices.

---

### Q5. Which markets (mandis) are consistently expensive?
- Identify mandis that regularly report higher prices.
- Useful for farmers when deciding where to sell their produce.

---

### Q6. Which commodities have stable prices versus highly volatile prices?
- Categorize commodities based on price stability.
- Helps balance risk and return for farmers and traders.

---

## Expected Outcome
The analysis will highlight high-value commodities, regional price disparities,
seasonal trends, market-level pricing behavior, and price stability,
providing actionable insights for agricultural stakeholders.
